## Why Air Fryers Are the Best Appliance for Frozen Foods {#why-air-fryers-best}

If you own an air fryer and you are still cooking frozen foods in your conventional oven, you are wasting time and getting worse results. That is not marketing — it is physics.

### How Air Fryers Transform Frozen Food

A conventional oven heats a large cavity slowly and unevenly. The vast interior means air circulation is weak, so frozen food releases moisture during thawing that reabsorbs into the breading. The result is soft, steamed exteriors rather than crispy ones.

Air fryers solve this with **rapid hot air circulation** inside a compact chamber. A powerful fan drives superheated air (375–400°F) directly across the food surface at high velocity. Three things happen simultaneously:

1. **Rapid surface defrosting** — The intense, close-range heat thaws the outer layer almost immediately
2. **Moisture evacuation** — Steam is carried away from the surface before it can soften breading
3. **Maillard reaction acceleration** — The dry, high-heat environment triggers browning and crisping far faster than a conventional oven

The compact basket design is actually an advantage here. Less dead air space means more efficient heat transfer to your food. A conventional oven has to heat cubic feet of empty space — an air fryer concentrates all that energy on the food itself.

### Air Fryer vs. Conventional Oven: Frozen Food Results

| Factor | Air Fryer | Conventional Oven |
|---|---|---|
| **Preheat time** | 2–3 minutes | 10–15 minutes |
| **Cook time** | 20–40% faster | Standard |
| **Crispiness** | Excellent — rivals deep frying | Often soft or uneven |
| **Energy use** | ~1,500W for 10–15 min | ~5,000W for 20–30 min |
| **Flip/shake needed** | Once, halfway | Often twice |
| **Batch size** | Smaller (2–4 servings) | Larger (full sheet pan) |

The trade-off is batch size. Air fryers cook smaller quantities at a time. But the speed difference means you can often run two air fryer batches in the time one oven batch takes — and both will be crispier.

For a deeper dive into how air fryers compare to traditional cooking methods, see our [air fryer vs deep fryer](/air-fryer-vs-deep-fryer) comparison.

### The 20-Minute Dinner Factor

Here is why frozen foods in an air fryer have become a weeknight staple for millions of households: you can go from freezer to plate in under 20 minutes with zero prep.

No thawing. No preheating a full oven. No oil to heat or dispose of. Pull the bag from the freezer, load the basket, press start. For busy families looking for [quick family meals in the air fryer](https://planfamilymeals.com), this changes the dinner equation entirely.

## Best Frozen Foods for Air Fryer — Ranked by Results {#best-frozen-foods-ranked}

Not all frozen foods benefit equally from air frying. After testing dozens of categories, here is our definitive ranking based on how much better the air fryer result is compared to conventional oven cooking.

### Tier 1: Exceptional Results (Air Fryer is Transformative)

<!-- IMG: Golden crispy french fries from an air fryer -->
![Perfectly golden crispy frozen french fries cooked in an air fryer showing ideal texture and color](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-fries-crispy.jpg)

#### 1. French Fries and Potato Products

**Air fryer advantage: Massive.** This is the single biggest upgrade over oven cooking. Oven fries are almost always disappointing — soft in places, unevenly browned, and lacking real crunch. Air fryer fries rival restaurant-quality deep-fried results.

The reason is simple: the intense, close-range airflow rapidly dehydrates the potato surface while the interior stays fluffy. You get a genuine crunch that oven cooking cannot replicate.

**Best brands we tested:** Ore-Ida Extra Crispy, McCain Quick Cook, Alexia Organic. All produced excellent results with no oil added.

#### 2. Chicken Nuggets and Tenders

<!-- IMG: Chicken nuggets and tenders on a plate with dipping sauces -->
![Air fryer chicken nuggets and tenders golden brown and crispy served on a white plate with dipping sauces](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-chicken-nuggets.jpg)

**Air fryer advantage: Very large.** Frozen chicken nuggets from an air fryer come out with an audible crunch — something the oven version almost never achieves. The breading stays intact and golden rather than going pale and soft.

Chicken tenders are even more impressive. The thicker pieces benefit from the rapid exterior crisping while staying juicy inside. The air fryer prevents the common oven problem of dry, overcooked interiors with undercooked breading.

**Best brands we tested:** Tyson Air Fried Nuggets, Bell & Evans Breaded Tenders, Perdue Simply Smart Organics.

#### 3. Pizza Rolls and Snack Bites

<!-- IMG: Before and after of pizza rolls air fried -->
![Frozen pizza rolls before and after air frying showing golden crispy transformation](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-pizza-rolls-before-after.jpg)

**Air fryer advantage: Dramatic.** Oven pizza rolls are famously inconsistent — some burst open while others are still cold inside. The air fryer cooks them evenly in under 8 minutes with crispy, blistered exteriors and molten filling.

The same applies to all small snack items: bagel bites, mini corn dogs, and frozen appetizer samplers. The air fryer is the definitive cooking method for this entire category.

### Tier 2: Excellent Results (Significantly Better Than Oven)

#### 4. Fish Sticks and Breaded Seafood

<!-- IMG: Fish sticks in air fryer basket -->
![Golden breaded fish sticks and fish fillets perfectly cooked in air fryer basket with lemon wedges](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-fish-sticks.jpg)

**Air fryer advantage: Large.** Fish sticks from an oven are often soggy on the bottom and barely golden on top. The air fryer crisps all sides evenly, and the fish inside stays moist and flaky rather than drying out.

Breaded shrimp performs exceptionally well too. The small size and high surface area are perfect for air frying.

**Important distinction:** This applies to **pre-breaded** frozen seafood only. Wet-battered items like beer-battered fish do not work in an air fryer — see the [foods to avoid section](#foods-to-avoid).

#### 5. Onion Rings

**Air fryer advantage: Large.** Frozen onion rings in a conventional oven collapse into sad, limp circles. The air fryer keeps them structurally intact with genuine crunch on the breading and sweet, tender onion inside.

A light oil spray before cooking makes a noticeable difference here. The breading on most frozen onion rings is relatively thin and benefits from the added fat.

#### 6. Mozzarella Sticks and Cheese-Filled Items

<!-- IMG: Mozzarella sticks and jalapeno poppers -->
![Air fryer mozzarella sticks and jalapeno poppers golden brown with stretchy cheese pull on a rustic board](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-appetizers.jpg)

**Air fryer advantage: Significant.** The key with cheese-filled items is timing. The air fryer crisps the breading fast enough that the cheese melts without completely liquefying and leaking out. Oven cooking takes longer, which means more cheese leakage.

**Tip:** Do not overcook mozzarella sticks. Check them 1 minute early on your first batch. Once the breading is golden, pull them immediately.

### Tier 3: Good Results (Noticeable Improvement Over Oven)

#### 7. Frozen Vegetables

<!-- IMG: Frozen vegetables in air fryer -->
![Frozen broccoli, cauliflower, and green beans cooked in air fryer with charred edges and bright colors](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-vegetables.jpg)

**Air fryer advantage: Moderate to good.** Frozen vegetables in an air fryer develop charred, caramelized edges that steaming or oven roasting cannot match. Broccoli and Brussels sprouts are the standouts — they get genuinely crispy tips.

The catch: you **must** use oil spray. Frozen vegetables without added fat will dry out and turn leathery rather than crispy. A 2-second spray of avocado or olive oil before cooking changes the result completely.

#### 8. Egg Rolls and Spring Rolls

**Air fryer advantage: Moderate.** The wrappers crisp up well, though not quite to deep-fried levels. The filling heats evenly, and you avoid the greasiness of deep frying. A light oil spray on the wrapper before cooking helps achieve a more authentic texture.

#### 9. Frozen Burritos and Hot Pockets

**Air fryer advantage: Moderate.** The air fryer gives these a toasted, crispy exterior that the microwave cannot achieve. The tortilla or dough shell gets genuine crunch while the filling heats through. Compared to the microwave (soggy) or oven (takes 25+ minutes), the air fryer hits the sweet spot.

#### 10. Frozen Waffles

**Air fryer advantage: Moderate.** Crispier than a toaster, more evenly cooked than an oven. Air fryer waffles develop a satisfying exterior crunch with a fluffy interior. Takes about the same time as a toaster but with noticeably better texture.

## Frozen Foods You Should Never Air Fry {#foods-to-avoid}

Not everything from the freezer belongs in an air fryer. These items either fail outright or produce significantly worse results than alternative cooking methods.

### Wet-Battered Foods

**Beer-battered fish, tempura vegetables, and any item with a liquid batter** will not work. The wet batter drips off the food and onto the basket or drip tray before it can set. You end up with unevenly coated food and a mess to clean up.

This is the fundamental limitation of air frying compared to [deep frying](/air-fryer-vs-deep-fryer). Wet batters require oil immersion to set the exterior instantly. Without that, the batter has nothing to hold it in place during cooking.

**Exception:** Pre-fried battered items that were already cooked in a factory (like some frozen fish and chips brands) do work because the batter has already been set.

### Frozen Soup Dumplings (Xiao Long Bao)

Soup dumplings are meant to contain liquid broth inside a delicate wrapper. The air fryer's intense dry heat evaporates the soup filling and dries out the wrapper. Steam them instead — that is the only method that preserves the broth.

### Large Frozen Roasts and Whole Poultry

A frozen 4-pound roast or whole chicken placed directly in an air fryer will have a charred exterior and frozen interior. The intense surface heat cooks the outside far faster than heat can penetrate to the centre.

Partial thawing (in the fridge for 24 hours) before air frying can work for some smaller cuts, but anything over 2 pounds should be fully thawed first.

### Foods with Very Loose or Fine Breading

Panko-crusted items with loose breadcrumbs can have the breading blown off by the air fryer fan. This applies mainly to homemade or artisanal frozen items — mass-produced frozen foods typically have breading that is firmly adhered during manufacturing.

If you encounter this issue, a light oil spray before cooking helps the breading stick.

### Leafy Greens (Frozen)

Frozen spinach, kale, or other leafy greens turn into dry, papery wisps in an air fryer. The high water content means they release moisture, then the thin leaves dehydrate and burn. Sauté or steam these instead.

## 7 Tips for Perfectly Crispy Frozen Foods Every Time {#crispy-tips}

These are the techniques that make the difference between "decent air fryer results" and "better than the restaurant."

### 1. Never Overcrowd the Basket

<!-- IMG: Comparison of overcrowded vs properly spaced basket -->
![Comparison of overcrowded air fryer basket versus properly spaced single layer of frozen foods](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-crowding-comparison.jpg)

This is the single most important rule. Overcrowding blocks airflow, which is the entire mechanism that makes air frying work. When food items touch each other or overlap, the contact points steam instead of crisp.

**The rule:** Every piece of food should have visible space around it. If you cannot see the basket bottom between pieces, you have too much food. Cook in batches instead — two properly spaced batches will finish faster and crispier than one overcrowded batch.

**Exception:** Small items like fries and tater tots can be loosely piled as long as you shake the basket every 4–5 minutes. They are small enough that shaking redistributes them and exposes new surfaces to airflow.

### 2. Shake or Flip Halfway Through

<!-- IMG: Person shaking air fryer basket -->
![Person shaking an air fryer basket halfway through cooking frozen foods to ensure even browning](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-shaking-basket.jpg)

Set a timer for the halfway point and shake the basket (for small items) or flip individual pieces (for larger items like fish fillets and chicken patties). This ensures even browning on all sides.

Some air fryers like the Cosori Pro II have a built-in Shake Reminder that beeps at the halfway point. If yours does not, use your phone timer.

### 3. Use a Light Oil Spray on Non-Breaded Items

Frozen vegetables, plain chicken pieces, and items without a pre-applied breading benefit enormously from a 1–2 second spray of cooking oil before air frying. The oil promotes browning and prevents drying.

**Best oils for air frying:** Avocado oil spray (highest smoke point), olive oil spray, or coconut oil spray. Avoid aerosol sprays with propellants — these can damage non-stick coatings. Use a refillable oil mister instead.

**Pre-breaded items** (nuggets, fish sticks, mozzarella sticks) do not need added oil. The breading already contains enough fat to crisp properly.

### 4. Preheat for 2–3 Minutes

Most air fryer manufacturers say preheating is optional. It is technically optional — but it makes a noticeable difference with frozen foods. A preheated air fryer starts crisping the food surface immediately rather than spending the first few minutes just warming up.

Set your air fryer to the target temperature and let it run empty for 2–3 minutes. Then add your frozen food. The difference is especially noticeable with fries and breaded items.

### 5. Do Not Thaw First (Usually)

Counterintuitively, most frozen foods perform **better** when cooked directly from frozen. Thawing breaded items first causes the exterior to absorb moisture and lose structural integrity. The breading gets soggy before it even hits the air fryer.

The exceptions are large items (anything over 1.5 pounds) and raw frozen meats that need to reach safe internal temperatures throughout. These benefit from refrigerator thawing before air frying.

### 6. Add Seasoning After Cooking (Not Before)

If you want to add extra seasoning to frozen foods, wait until after cooking. Dry seasonings applied to frozen surfaces fall off during cooking as ice melts and creates steam. Season after cooking when the surface is hot, dry, and slightly oily — the seasonings will stick.

**Exception:** A light oil spray before cooking can carry seasoning. Spray, then season, then cook. This works well for frozen fries and vegetables.

### 7. Use Parchment Liners for Easy Cleanup

Pre-cut parchment paper liners designed for air fryers catch drips and crumbs without blocking airflow. They have perforated holes that allow air circulation while keeping your basket clean. This is especially useful for cheese-filled items like mozzarella sticks and pizza that can leak.

**Important:** Only use parchment liners when food is in the basket. An empty liner can blow up into the heating element.

## Common Mistakes and How to Fix Them {#common-mistakes}

### Mistake 1: Using the Package Directions

Frozen food packaging gives oven instructions, not air fryer instructions. If you follow the oven temperature and time, you will overcook your food. Air fryers cook 20–30% faster than conventional ovens.

**Fix:** Reduce the package temperature by 25°F and start checking 3–5 minutes before the package time. Use our [temperature chart](#temperature-time-chart) as your starting point.

### Mistake 2: Not Cleaning Between Batches

Crumbs and grease from one batch burn during the next batch, creating smoke and off-flavors. This is especially problematic when cooking different foods back to back — nobody wants fish-flavored fries.

**Fix:** Give the basket a quick shake over the trash between batches. For heavier residue, a 30-second wipe with a damp paper towel is enough. For a deeper clean between cooking sessions, see our guide on [how to clean an air fryer](/how-to-clean-air-fryer).

### Mistake 3: Ignoring the Drip Tray

The drip tray or bottom pan catches grease and moisture. If it fills up, smoke and grease fires become a risk. More commonly, accumulated moisture in the drip tray creates steam that rises back up into the basket and makes your food less crispy.

**Fix:** Check and empty the drip tray when cooking fatty items like chicken wings or sausages. Lining it with foil makes cleanup instant.

### Mistake 4: Cooking Everything at 400°F

Higher is not always better. Delicate items like mozzarella sticks, fish, and vegetables cook better at 370–380°F. Blasting them at 400°F chars the exterior before the interior heats through. Reserve 400°F for items that benefit from aggressive crisping — fries, tater tots, and onion rings.

### Mistake 5: Opening the Basket Too Often

Every time you pull the basket out, you lose heat and extend cooking time. Check once at the halfway point to shake or flip, and once near the end to check doneness. That is enough.

**Exception:** When trying a new food for the first time, checking at the 75% mark helps you dial in the timing without risking overcooking.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Do you need to thaw frozen food before air frying?

No — air fryers are designed to cook from frozen. The rapid hot air circulation defrosts and crisps simultaneously. Thawing breaded items beforehand often makes them soggy because the exterior absorbs moisture during thawing.

The only exception is large items (over 1.5 pounds) like whole chicken pieces or roasts. These should be partially thawed in the refrigerator for even cooking throughout.

### What temperature is best for frozen foods in an air fryer?

Most frozen foods cook best between **375–400°F (190–200°C)**. Here is the quick breakdown:

- **400°F:** Fries, tater tots, onion rings (maximum crispness)
- **380–390°F:** Chicken nuggets, fish sticks, egg rolls (balanced cooking)
- **370–375°F:** Mozzarella sticks, vegetables, delicate items (gentle crisping)

Start 25°F lower than oven package directions and adjust from there.

### Why is my air fried frozen food not crispy?

Three common causes:

1. **Overcrowded basket** — This is the most common reason. Overlapping food blocks airflow and creates steaming instead of crisping. Use a single layer with space between pieces.
2. **No oil spray** — Non-breaded items need a light spray of cooking oil to promote browning. Without it, surfaces dry out and turn leathery instead of crispy.
3. **Skipped the halfway shake** — Food touching the basket on one side gets crispy while the other side stays soft. Shake or flip at the halfway mark.

### Can you stack frozen foods in an air fryer?

It depends on the item. Small items like fries, tater tots, and popcorn chicken can be loosely stacked because shaking the basket every 4–5 minutes redistributes them. Larger items like chicken patties, fish fillets, and burgers must be in a strict single layer — stacking these leads to raw centres and soggy contact points.

For maximum results, a single layer with visible gaps between every piece always produces the best outcome regardless of food type.

### How long do frozen french fries take in an air fryer?

Thin-cut fries take **12–15 minutes at 400°F**, while thick-cut steak fries take **15–20 minutes**. Shake the basket at the halfway point for even browning. No oil is required, but a light spray produces noticeably crispier results.

Crinkle-cut and curly fries cook slightly faster (10–14 minutes) due to their increased surface area.

### What frozen foods should you NOT put in an air fryer?

These items consistently fail in an air fryer:

- **Wet-battered foods** (beer-battered fish, tempura) — the liquid batter drips off before setting
- **Frozen soup dumplings** — the broth evaporates and the wrapper dries out
- **Large frozen roasts** (over 4 lbs) — charred exterior with frozen interior
- **Foods with very loose breading** — the fan blows the crumbs off
- **Frozen leafy greens** (spinach, kale) — they dehydrate and burn

Anything that requires full oil immersion to cook properly — like authentic deep-fried battered fish — will not work in an air fryer.
