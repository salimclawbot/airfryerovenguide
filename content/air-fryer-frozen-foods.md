## Why Air Fryers Excel at Frozen Foods {#why-air-fryers-excel}

Air fryers are arguably the single best appliance for cooking frozen foods — better than a conventional oven and approaching deep fryer quality for most items. Here's why:

### The Science Behind It

Conventional ovens heat food slowly and unevenly. The large cavity means air circulation is weak, so frozen food often comes out with a soft, steamed exterior rather than a crispy one. The moisture released during thawing has nowhere to go, and it reabsorbs into the breading.

Air fryers solve this with **rapid hot air circulation** at close range. The compact cooking chamber and powerful fan move superheated air (190–200°C) directly across the food surface at high speed. This does three things simultaneously:

1. **Rapid defrosting** — The intense heat thaws the outer layer quickly
2. **Moisture removal** — Steam is carried away from the food surface before it can soften breading
3. **Maillard reaction** — The dry, high-heat environment triggers browning and crisping

The result is frozen food that tastes closer to deep-fried than oven-baked — without the oil, mess, or preheating wait.

### Time Savings

Air fryers cook frozen foods approximately **25–40% faster** than a conventional oven. No preheating required for most models, and the compact chamber reaches temperature in under 3 minutes versus 10–15 minutes for an oven.

## Best Frozen Foods for Air Fryer (Ranked) {#best-frozen-foods}

Not all frozen foods perform equally in an air fryer. Here's what works best, ranked by how much improvement you'll see over conventional oven cooking.

### 1. French Fries and Potato Products — A+

![Golden crispy french fries freshly cooked in air fryer](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-fries.jpg)

Frozen fries are the **single best frozen food** for air fryers. The difference between oven-baked fries and air-fried fries is dramatic — genuinely crispy exterior with a fluffy interior, close to deep-fried quality.

**Why they work so well**: Potatoes have high starch content that crisps beautifully in rapid dry heat. The thin shape means maximum surface area exposed to circulating hot air.

**Best results**: Shake the basket halfway through, don't overcrowd (single layer), and a light oil spray takes them from good to excellent. Tater tots and hash browns perform equally well.

### 2. Chicken Nuggets and Tenders — A+

![Golden crispy chicken nuggets freshly cooked in air fryer with one cut open showing juicy interior](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-chicken.jpg)

Frozen chicken nuggets and tenders come out noticeably better from an air fryer than a conventional oven. The breading gets genuinely crunchy rather than just warm, and the chicken inside stays juicier because the faster cooking time means less moisture loss.

**Key tip**: Flip halfway through for even browning on both sides. The bottom of the nugget sitting against the basket won't brown as well without flipping.

### 3. Fish Sticks — A

![Crispy golden fish sticks cooked in air fryer with lemon wedge and tartar sauce](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-fish.jpg)

Fish sticks and breaded fish fillets are excellent in an air fryer. The breading crisps up firmly while the fish inside steams gently in its own moisture. A light oil spray before cooking significantly improves the golden colour and crunch.

**Watch for**: Thin fish sticks cook fast — check at 8 minutes to avoid drying out. Use a meat thermometer for thicker fillets (internal temp should reach 63°C / 145°F).

### 4. Pizza Rolls and Snack Bites — A

![Hot pizza rolls freshly cooked in air fryer, golden brown with melted cheese visible](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-pizza.jpg)

Pizza rolls, mozzarella sticks, jalapeño poppers, and similar frozen snacks cook exceptionally well. The air fryer produces an evenly crispy shell without the sogginess that often comes from oven baking.

**Warning**: Mozzarella sticks are time-sensitive — even 1–2 minutes too long and the cheese melts through the breading. Start checking at 6 minutes.

### 5. Frozen Vegetables — A-

![Roasted frozen vegetables from air fryer with golden caramelised edges](/images/articles/air-fryer-frozen-foods-vegetables.jpg)

This is the sleeper pick. Most people don't think to air fry frozen vegetables, but the results are significantly better than steaming or microwaving. Air frying produces **caramelised edges** and a roasted texture that makes frozen broccoli, cauliflower, and Brussels sprouts genuinely enjoyable.

**Essential step**: Toss frozen vegetables in 1–2 teaspoons of oil and seasoning before air frying. Without oil, they'll dry out rather than caramelise. Salt, garlic powder, and parmesan is a reliable combination.

### 6. Egg Rolls and Spring Rolls — A-

Frozen egg rolls and spring rolls crisp up beautifully in the air fryer, producing a crackling shell similar to deep-fried results. A light oil spray on the wrapper before cooking is essential — without it, the wrapper can dry out and become tough rather than crispy.

### 7. Frozen Breakfast Items — B+

Frozen waffles, breakfast burritos, and sausage patties all work well. Waffles in particular come out crispier than a toaster, though they take slightly longer (5–7 minutes at 175°C). Breakfast burritos heat evenly without the hot-outside-frozen-inside problem of microwaving.

## Frozen Foods to Avoid in an Air Fryer {#foods-to-avoid}

Some frozen foods genuinely don't work well. Save yourself the disappointment:

### Wet-Battered Items

Beer-battered fish, tempura vegetables, and anything with a liquid coating before freezing should not go in an air fryer. The wet batter drips through the basket before it can set, creating a mess and leaving the food with patchy, uneven coating. These require deep frying for the oil to set the batter instantly.

### Large Frozen Roasts

A frozen whole chicken or large roast will not cook evenly in an air fryer. The exterior overcooks and dries out before the centre reaches a safe temperature. Partially thaw large items first or use an oven.

### Very Loose Breading

Some frozen foods have light, powdery coatings that blow off in the air fryer's powerful fan. If the breading isn't firmly adhered, it ends up at the bottom of the basket instead of on the food.

### High-Liquid Items

Frozen soup dumplings, items in sauce packets, and anything designed to be boiled or steamed won't work. The air fryer is a dry-heat cooking method — liquid-based frozen foods need moisture to cook properly.

### Foods That Need Oil Immersion

Donuts, churros, and funnel cake fries require full submersion in hot oil. The oil is part of the cooking process, not just a heat transfer medium. Air-fried donuts taste like bread, not donuts.

## Tips for Perfectly Crispy Results {#crispy-tips}

These techniques make the difference between mediocre and exceptional air-fried frozen food.

### 1. Single Layer Is Non-Negotiable

The most common mistake with frozen foods is overcrowding the basket. Air fryers work by circulating hot air around every surface of the food. Stack items two or three deep, and the middle pieces steam rather than crisp.

**Rule of thumb**: If you can't see basket surface between the food items, you've got too much in there. Cook in batches — it's still faster than an oven.

### 2. Shake or Flip Halfway Through

Set a timer for the halfway point. Shake the basket (for small items like fries and tots) or flip individual pieces (for larger items like chicken patties and fish fillets). This ensures even browning on all sides and prevents the bottom from getting soggy where it contacts the basket.

### 3. Light Oil Spray Makes a Big Difference

Many frozen foods already contain oil in their coating, but a 1–2 second spray of cooking oil before air frying noticeably improves crispness and colour. This is especially important for:

- Frozen vegetables (essential — they have no coating)
- Breaded fish (improves golden colour)
- Sweet potato fries (prevents drying)
- Egg rolls and spring rolls (crisps the wrapper)

Use a pump-style oil sprayer rather than aerosol cans — some aerosol cooking sprays contain additives that damage non-stick basket coatings over time.

### 4. Don't Thaw First

Cook frozen foods directly from frozen. Thawing introduces excess moisture that makes breading soggy and prevents proper crisping. The only exceptions are large items (over 1kg) that need partial thawing for even cooking.

### 5. Preheat for Thick Items Only

For thin, single-layer items like fries and nuggets, preheating is optional — the short preheat time is negligible. For thicker items like chicken patties and stuffed items, a 3-minute preheat at cooking temperature gives better results.

### 6. Use Parchment Liners for Cheese Items

Mozzarella sticks, pizza, and anything with exposed cheese can drip through the basket. Perforated parchment liners (with holes for airflow) prevent mess while maintaining crispness. Never use solid parchment — the holes are essential.

## Common Mistakes and Troubleshooting {#troubleshooting}

### Food Is Crispy Outside but Frozen Inside

The temperature is too high. Lower by 10°C and add 2–3 minutes to cooking time. This happens most often with thick items like stuffed chicken or large fish fillets. Using a meat thermometer is the only reliable way to confirm the centre is cooked.

### Breading Falls Off

Two causes: the basket wasn't clean (old grease prevents adhesion), or you're shaking too vigorously. For delicate breaded items, flip with tongs rather than shaking. A light oil spray also helps breading adhere.

### Uneven Cooking

Check that you're not blocking the air intake vents at the back of the unit. Also ensure food isn't touching the sides of the basket. If one side consistently cooks faster, your air fryer may have a hot spot — rotate the basket 180° at the halfway point.

### Smoke Coming from the Air Fryer

This is usually grease hitting the heating element. Add a tablespoon of water to the bottom tray (below the basket) to catch dripping fat. This is common when cooking fatty frozen items like sausage rolls or chicken wings. It's not dangerous, but the smoke can set off alarms.

For detailed maintenance advice, see our complete guide on [how to clean an air fryer](/how-to-clean-air-fryer).

### Food Is Dry

You're overcooking. Reduce time by 2 minutes and check. Frozen foods continue cooking for 30–60 seconds after removal due to residual heat. Also consider adding a light oil spray — dryness often comes from insufficient surface fat.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

**Do you need to thaw frozen food before air frying?**

No. Air fryers are designed to cook food from frozen. The rapid hot air circulation defrosts and crisps simultaneously. Thawing first can actually make breaded items soggy because excess moisture softens the coating before cooking begins. The only exception is large frozen items like whole chickens — these should be partially thawed for even cooking.

**What temperature should I set my air fryer for frozen foods?**

Most frozen foods cook best between 190–200°C (375–400°F). Breaded items like chicken nuggets and fish sticks do well at 190°C, while fries and potato products benefit from 200°C for maximum crispness. Delicate items like frozen vegetables work best at 175–180°C to prevent burning before they heat through.

**Why is my air fried frozen food not crispy?**

The three most common causes are overcrowding the basket (blocks airflow), not using a light oil spray (especially on non-breaded items), and not shaking or flipping halfway through cooking. A single layer with space between items is essential for crispy results.

**Can you put aluminium foil in an air fryer?**

Yes, but only in the basket — never covering the bottom where airflow vents are located. Foil can help with cleanup and prevent cheese or sauces from dripping through the basket. Always weigh it down with food so it doesn't blow into the heating element.

**How long do frozen french fries take in an air fryer?**

Thin-cut frozen fries take 12–15 minutes at 200°C (400°F), while thick-cut or steak fries take 15–20 minutes. Shake the basket at the halfway point for even browning. No oil is needed but a light spray produces noticeably crispier results.

**What frozen foods should you NOT put in an air fryer?**

Avoid wet-battered items (beer-battered fish, tempura), large frozen roasts over 2kg, foods with very loose breading that will blow off in the fan, and items with high liquid content like frozen soup dumplings. Anything requiring full oil immersion will not work in an air fryer.
